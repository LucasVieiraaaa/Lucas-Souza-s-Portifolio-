System of Information (PUC-MG)


•Fundamentals of Computing: Students are introduced to the fundamental principles of computing, including programming, algorithms, data structures, and programming logic.
•Information Systems: The course covers the theory and practice of information systems, including analysis, design, implementation and management of information systems in organizations.
•Software Development: Students learn to design, develop, and test software across multiple platforms and programming languages. This includes web, mobile and desktop application development.
•Database: The course covers database concepts, data modeling, query languages ​​(SQL), and database management, preparing students to handle large volumes of data effectively.
•Computer Networks: Students study the fundamentals of computer networks, communication protocols, network security, and network systems administration.
•Information Technology Management: The course also includes aspects of IT management, covering topics such as IT governance, project management, strategies and legal and ethical aspects.
